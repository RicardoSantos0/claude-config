# Domain Context: Content Creation

## Core Principles
- Audience first: content is designed for a specific reader/viewer, not the creator
- Clarity over cleverness: clear communication beats sophisticated language
- Consistency: tone, style, and terminology must be consistent across all pieces
- Accuracy: factual claims must be verified before publication

## Quality Standards
- Editorial review for any public-facing content
- Style guide adherence: defined voice, tone, formatting standards
- Accessibility: readable at appropriate literacy level; alt text for images; captions for video
- SEO alignment: metadata, structure, and keywords aligned to distribution channel
- Content brief required before production begins (goals, audience, format, length, deadline)

## Common Risks
- Audience mismatch: content pitched at wrong reading level or assumed knowledge
- Factual errors: unverified claims that damage credibility
- Brand inconsistency: conflicting tone/messaging across channels
- Copyright and attribution: plagiarism or unattributed sources
- Legal risk: claims about competitors, medical/legal/financial advice without disclaimers
- Staleness: content becomes outdated without a review/update schedule

## Best Practices
- Content calendar for planning and consistency across channels
- Structured review process: author → editor → subject matter expert → legal (if needed) → publish
- Modular content: reusable building blocks reduce production time and maintain consistency
- Versioning for evergreen content that needs regular updates
- Performance metrics defined before publishing (goal: what does success look like?)
- Localization considerations for multi-market content

## Prior Art
- Inverted pyramid for news-style content (most important information first)
- AIDA framework for marketing content (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action)
- Plain Language guidelines for government/enterprise communications
- Chicago Manual of Style / AP Style Guide for editorial standards
