# Domain Context: Research

## Core Principles
- Rigor: conclusions must follow from evidence; distinguish correlation from causation
- Transparency: methodology must be documented completely enough to replicate
- Objectivity: acknowledge limitations and contradictory evidence
- Peer review: findings should be reviewed by domain peers before being acted upon

## Quality Standards
- Research questions must be specific and falsifiable
- Methodology section must be complete before data collection begins
- Sample size and statistical power calculated before study execution
- Contradictory findings must be reported, not suppressed

## Common Risks
- Confirmation bias: collecting evidence that confirms the hypothesis only
- Publication bias: only reporting positive findings
- P-hacking: testing multiple hypotheses until one is "significant"
- Scope creep from "interesting tangents" that dilute the core question
- Findings over-generalized beyond the study's actual scope

## Best Practices
- Pre-registration: document hypotheses before data collection
- Multiple reviewers for any finding that will drive significant decisions
- Distinguish exploratory research (hypothesis generation) from confirmatory (hypothesis testing)
- Literature review required before starting new research (check prior art)
- Research plan approved by stakeholders before execution

## Prior Art
- Systematic reviews and meta-analyses over single studies for high-stakes decisions
- Mixed methods (quantitative + qualitative) for complex questions
- Triangulation: confirm findings through multiple independent methods
- Research ethics review for studies involving human participants or sensitive data
